Tennis Enthusiast

I am passionate about tennis, a sport I have loved since I was six years old. My dream was to win Wimbledon, inspired by my idol Roger Federer. While I didn't reach such heights, I did achieve significant milestones:

  • Ranked among the top 400 players in Switzerland
  • Participated in the national championships at the junior level
  • Won the Under 16s championship of Central Switzerland
  • Trained professionally for four months at the JC Ferrero Equelite Sport Academy in Spain

Tennis has shaped my life in many ways, teaching me valuable life lessons:

Life Lessons from Tennis

1. Leadership

For over a decade, I have been part of the interclub tennis scene, a team competition in Switzerland. Each match consists of six singles and three doubles. As the team captain, my responsibilities include organizing the team, deciding the lineup, and ensuring we are prepared for each match. This role has honed my skills in team communication and decision-making, culminating in our promotion to the first league.

2. Fairness and Honesty

In amateur tennis, players make their own line calls. This requires a high level of integrity, as it can be tempting to cheat. I believe that true victory comes from fairness and honesty. This principle extends beyond the court, teaching me to react to situations with integrity and focus on what I can control.

3. Practice Makes Better

Tennis is a sport that demands technical precision, requiring countless hours of practice. Training at the JC Ferrero Equelite Sport Academy taught me the value of hard work, discipline, and continuous improvement. While perfection is elusive, practice leads to significant progress and enjoyment.

4. Mental Resilience and Patience

Success in tennis often hinges on mental strength. Staying positive during tough matches and maintaining focus is crucial. These experiences have equipped me with the resilience to handle stress and stay optimistic in everyday life.

5. The Joy of Tennis

Tennis is not only a challenging sport but also a source of immense fun. My mantra:

"Enter and leave the court with a smile. Did you win? Then smile, you did everything right today. Did you lose? Then smile, your opponent showed you what you can improve on."
