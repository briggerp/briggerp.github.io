Globetrotter

I am privileged to have grown up in two countries in the USA and Switzerland. This has naturally led to me grow up with travels and given me the chance to see a lot of the world at a young age. It wasn't however until my exchange semester in Singapore that I genuinely discovered my passion for traveling. My exchange semester marked my first time in Asia. After my exchange, I backpacked for three months across Southeast Asia. My experience was enriching, mind-opening and unforgettable. Traveling has brought me much more than just beautiful vistas and a sense of freedom. It has also brought me: 1. Appreciation of what I have 2. Human diversity is beautiful 3. The world is big 1. Appreciation of what I have It is a classic that you only miss what you have when it is gone. I believe this is especially true in a materialistic sense. Whilst traveling, I miss the clean tap water, fresh mountain air and snowy mountain peaks of Switzerland. I dearly miss my parents, brothers and friends. Yet, when I spent a couple nights in the depths of the Sumatran jungle, cooking food and boiling river water to kill of bacteria on a wood fire as well as sleeping in a tent, I realized that happiness and joy don't just depend on materialistic things but very much on experiences. Hearing a hornbill soar across the sky or watching an Orang-Utan carry its infant was mesmerizing. Traveling has made me appreciate everything I have in my life. Most importantly, it showed me to appreciate what you can always have, which is your mind and thoughts. 2. Human diversity is beautiful What I want to get across: I love traveling because it's eye-opening Makes you appreciate what you have Also shows you that things can be done differently, with more or less success Makes you be a good planner Humanity is beautiful in all its diversity
