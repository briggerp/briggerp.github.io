ETH Zurich

Master Thesis: Data-Driven Control

My Master's thesis focused on data selection for Data-EnablEd Predictive Control (DeePC) in urban traffic control. DeePC was shown to reduce traffic congestion and emissions more effectively than traditional MPC controllers. However, it is highly sensitive to the data it uses. My thesis aimed to determine which data should be used under varying traffic conditions to ensure the effectiveness of DeePC. This research is part of the REAL initiative of NCCR Automation, where I regularly present my findings to enhance my presentation skills and learn to convey information concisely to diverse audiences. Learn more about the REAL initiative.

Semester Project: Imitation Learning

For my semester project, I developed a neural network to control a motorized labyrinth game by imitating a Model-Predictive Control (MPC) expert. This imitation learning approach allowed for higher bandwidth control with a similar success rate. Through this project, I gained experience with machine learning libraries such as PyTorch and TensorFlow, and furthered my knowledge of ROS2. Computational Control

The Computational Control course covered various control methods for systems where a simple PID controller is insufficient. I learned about model-based and data-based controllers such as MPC, LQR, and DeePC, as well as learning-based controllers like Q-learning and SARSA. For the final project, I programmed MPC and DeePC controllers to autonomously land a rocket. This project involved using the reinforcement learning API Gym and solving optimization problems with cvxpy. View the rocket project report (PDF)

Path Planning and Decision Making for Autonomous Robots

In this course, I implemented various algorithms for path planning and decision making in Python. These included Depth-First and Breadth-First graph search, Uniform Cost search, A* search, policy and value iteration for Markov Decision Processes, and Linear Programming for optimization. The final project involved developing a path planning and decision-making pipeline for multiple vehicles on a road network with obstacles. I modeled the car dynamics and used RRT* for path planning, ensuring vehicles avoided collisions.
