Focus Project Griffin

In the third year of my Bachelor's, I participated in the Focus Project Griffin. Over the course of a year, our team of eight built an aerial manipulator with a parallel robotic arm from the ground up. This experience was mesmerizing in many ways. We shaped the vision and goals of the project, similar to working for a startup in its earliest stage. We pitched our project to companies to gather funds and material sponsors, and I enhanced my presentation skills through weekly update meetings with our supervisors. It was amazing to see our drone evolve from a paper sketch to a CAD model to a functional aerial system. As the team lead, I ensured we met our weekly goals and maintained high spirits during challenging times. I worked on modeling the system dynamics, deriving aerodynamic equations for our rotor configuration, and controlling our flying base. We wrote our code in C++ and Python, and used ROS for robot communication. Before testing on the actual hardware, we tested our drone in the Gazebo simulation environment. For the real hardware, I learned how to tune our motor controllers and conduct force-torque measurements. Lastly, I acquired experience with laser cutters, 3D printers, and basic soldering and wiring skills.
