Focus Project Griffin

Project Info

In the third year of my Bachelor's, I participated in the Focus Project Griffin. Over the course of a year, our team of eight built an aerial manipulator with a parallel robotic arm from the ground up. This experience was mesmerizing in many ways. We shaped the vision and goals of the project, similar to working for a startup in its earliest stage. We pitched our project to companies to gather funds and material sponsors, and I enhanced my presentation skills through weekly update meetings with our supervisors. It was amazing to see our drone evolve from a paper sketch to a CAD model to a functional aerial system. As the team lead, I ensured we met our weekly goals and maintained high spirits during challenging times. Furthermore, I worked on modelling and controlling the system. The project improved my C++, python, CAD, ROS and git coding skills. I learned how to configure a Gazebo simulation environment, and how to work with 3D printers, lasercutters, how to solder wires and how to tune the embedded motor controllers.

Conference Paper

After completing the project, we published a conference paper at the International Conference on Unmanned Aerial Systems (ICUAS). The paper describes our novel mechanical design and was given the Best Paper award at the conference.

Aerial Manipulation

Our aerial manipulator combines an omnidirectional floating base with a parallel robot arm. The coaxial, tiltable propeller groups allow the robot fly in any pose. The robot arm compensates for positional errors of the floating base and grasp objects with adaptive fingers. The videos below showcase two grasping missions.
